COURSE ASSIGNMENTS

Assignment #1: SOUND EFFECTS: FIELD RECORDING

Assigned 9/9, Due 9/20

The natural world around us (including... well, "us") is an amazing source of sound material.

Using a portable audio recorder, your assignment is to create a set of recorded samples that represent "found" sounds that could be re-purposed into the sonic fabric of your game. For the purposes of this assignment we will be using Unity's built-in sound capabilities such as Audio Clips and Audio Sources, trigger volumes and continuous/environmental data..

To convert your raw material into musical and sonic effects that "fit" your game space, explore the capabilities of Audacity to process your sound files in creative and transformative ways.

Using these sounds pulled from everyday life (i.e. not downloaded from freesounds.org or ripped from your music collection), create a set of sound and musical effects for _either_ the Roll-a-Ball or spaceship demos from class, OR for a small game experience of your own design.

To successfully carry out this assignment please follow these instructions:

  1. Check out a portable audio recorder (and microphone if necessary) from the HASS Equipment Room (SAGE 1112).
  2. Familiarize yourself with its operation, specifically how to set the input level, how to record, how to play back your recording on the device, how to copy your files from the device (via USB) to your computer.
  3. Document the source of your recordings: this can be in the form of photos or video of your recording location/configuration, or as a written description of the material/location being recorded.

Please use sound in a variety of functional roles in your game, including (but not limited to):

  1. Discretized sound events (like jumping, firing, pickup triggers, keyboard/mouse-button events)
  2. Longer or continuous/looping sound events
  3. "Background" music
  4. Think about creating multiple samples for a given event and choosing different samples either randomly or based on gameplay values.

Please turn in the following:

  1. Your set of samples: both your original "clean" sounds and your processed/manipulated sounds.
  2. A simple narrative description of each sample and how it is used in the game.

You can contact the equipment room staff at (518) 276-4775, or online at hasshelp [at] rpi [dot] edu.

For the purposes of this assignment, please keep the following suggestions in mind:

  • * Set the recorder to record at a high sampling rate (e.g. 44.1kHz) and a bit rate of 16 bit or higher.
  • * Make sure the input level for the microphone is set high enough to capture the type of sound that has drawn your attention.
  • * Test your setup early and often. TAPE IS CHEAP. If you're not absolutely sure you captured the sound the way you would like, listen to your recording on the spot. Iterate until it sounds the way you'd like.
  • * Please respect the privacy of people or material that might wander into your recording. In general, distinct voices or audible musical sources (i.e. radios, music in general) might not be the best source material for this exercise.

Assignment #2: SOUND ROOM PROPOSAL

Assigned 9/23, Due 9/27

To correspond with the New Atlantis project's October 10 concert, we will be designing and building a series of music and sound environments for real-time network performance. As a group we can decide what kinds of sonic and visual experiences we wish to contribute to the project, either a few large ideas or a larger number of smaller ideas.

For assignment #2, please design a sonic and visual interaction that you would like to propose the class work on for the concert. The specs require the project run within Unity and for the time being, they should use the built-in audio features of Unity (i.e. no PD yet).

In a written (basic) game design document, propose a "Sound Room" for inclusion in the New Atlantis project. Please describe your proposed idea, including if possible, visual sketches, descriptions of the proposed interactions, and descriptions or links to reference audio material.

A sample (more advanced) game design document used in the Experimental Game Design class can be found here.

Your design should include the following:

  1. A high-level overview of your idea.
  2. Details about how you envision the user interactions to work with Unity

Please keep in mind that the goal of this document is to make your ideas clear to your classmates, so that we may all choose projects to work on for next week.

Assignment #4: Sound-Only Game

Assigned 10/28, Due 11/11

For this assignment, build an interactive music game where the primary player interaction and gameplay is conveyed via sound and music. For this project your audio engine should be built within Pure Data.

The game can have no visible user-interface or a minimal user-interface, the choice is yours. What is important is that information you wish to convey to your players in primarily conveyed via sound and music.

Your game can be built entirely in Pure Data or you can use Pure Data as the audio backend to a front end built in Unity, MobMuPlat, or any other OSC-capable interface.
